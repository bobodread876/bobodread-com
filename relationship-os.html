The first build

Built for one wealth manager. Not configured — built.

My cousin works in wealth management. His business is allocators, founders, connectors, and clients — and none of it fit the tools he tried. Salesforce wanted a sales team he doesn't have. The personal-CRM apps wanted him to work their way. So his network lived in his head, his phone, and a spreadsheet he didn't trust.

Over one weekend I built him Meridian: a relationship operating system shaped around how he actually works.

His five cohorts — Capital, Companies, Opportunities, Influence, Clients — are the vocabulary of the software, not labels bolted onto someone else's pipeline. His year-end targets are the scorecard. His morning digest arrives at 8:00 AM his time. That precision is what a bespoke build buys.

What a build includes

Everything a network needs to compound

One-click capture

Viewing a profile on LinkedIn, one click files the person — AI reads the page, classifies them (Founder, Capital Allocator, Family Office, Connector…), and pulls role, company, photo, and a one-line summary into the right cohort.

A “Today” engine

Who to talk to today and why: relationship-strength scores, silence alarms, priority conversations, and the introductions worth making.

Voice notes → memory

Talk for thirty seconds after a meeting. The system files who you met, what changed, and what to follow up on — before you forget it.

Your categories, your targets

Cohorts, scoring, and a green-light scorecard measured against your own year-end goals — not a template's.

A daily digest

Every morning, a push notification with the day's priorities. A weekly review shows what your effort actually changed.

Phone and desktop

An installable app that syncs both ways. Capture on the laptop, act from the pocket.
