A Relationship OS, built to fit

← bobodread.com

My cousin works in wealth management. His business runs on relationships — allocators, founders, connectors, clients — and none of it fit the tools he tried. Salesforce wanted a sales team he doesn't have. The personal-CRM apps wanted him to work their way. So his network lived in his head, his phone, and a spreadsheet he didn't trust.

Over a weekend I built him Meridian: a relationship operating system shaped around how he actually works. Not configured. Built.

What his copy does

  • His five cohorts, not generic pipelines — Capital, Companies, Opportunities, Influence, Clients. The vocabulary of his business, in the software.
  • One-click LinkedIn capture — viewing a profile, one click files the person: AI reads the page, classifies them (Founder, Capital Allocator, Family Office, Connector…), pulls role, company, photo, and a one-line summary into the right cohort. One profile at a time, deliberately — curation, not bulk scraping.
  • A "Today" engine — who to talk to today and why: relationship strength scores, silence alarms, priority conversations, introductions worth making.
  • Voice notes in, structured memory out — he talks after a meeting; the AI files who he met, what changed, and what to follow up on.
  • A daily digest push at 8:00 AM and a week-in-review that shows what his effort actually changed.
  • A scorecard against his own year-end targets — total relationships, allocators, meetings captured, introductions made.
  • Phone and desktop — installable app, synced both ways.
